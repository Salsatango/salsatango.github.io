In cinema and theatre, a "pregnant pause" is a moment of long silence that invites anticipation.
It adds significance to the words that follow. It gives gravity to the dialogue, as the
character seems deep in thought.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
According to the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition (DSM-5),
characteristics such as:
>>“Failure to initiate or respond to social interactions,
>>Poorly integrated verbal and nonverbal communication,
>>Abnormalities in eye contact and body language or
>>Deficits in understanding and use of gestures”
are symptoms that factor in a diagnosis of many disorders, notably Autism Spectrum Disorder.
In the movie "Drive" (2011), Ryan Gosling plays a "cool" garage mechanic/getaway driver who
maintains a stoic facade. He is avaricious with words, and uses
pregnant pauses anytime he can. However, many viewers characterize the Driver as off-putting.
How to interpret Ryan Gosling's slow line delivery?
Does his long silences and hesitations make his character
deep and mysterious, or does it seem ridiculous or even pathological?
